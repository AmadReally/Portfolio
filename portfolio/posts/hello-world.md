# Hello World — and Why I Still Mean It

Every developer's first line is `Hello, World!`

Most people treat it as a throwaway — a ritual you do once and forget.
I think about it differently.

## It's not just a print statement

Every time I open a new project, a blank file, a fresh terminal — I'm saying hello to the world again. A new chance to build something that didn't exist yesterday. A new chance to leave something behind that might matter to someone.

That's the thing nobody tells you about coding.

It's not really about the code.

It's about the **belief** that you can shape reality with nothing but logic and stubbornness.

## What keeps me here

Late nights. Cold coffee. Bugs that make no sense at 2am and suddenly resolve themselves at 3.

I keep coming back because there's nothing like the feeling of something *working* — when the screen finally shows what you imagined in your head, and for a moment the world feels exactly the right size.

Code is the closest thing I know to magic.
You write words, and things happen.
You write *better* words, and things happen *beautifully*.
