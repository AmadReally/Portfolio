# Joining BurhanDev — People, Code, and a New Chapter

I didn't expect a dev environment to feel like home.

But here we are.

## The beginning

When I joined BurhanDev, I thought it was just another place to write code.
Ship features. Close tickets. Repeat.

I was wrong.

What I found was a room full of people who actually *care* — about the craft, about each other, about building things that matter. The kind of energy that makes you open your laptop on a Saturday not because you have to, but because you genuinely want to see what happens next.

That's rare. I don't take it lightly.

## Moon & Matnep

Some people teach you through documentation and pull request reviews.

Moon and Matnep taught me by just *being around*.

Watching the way they approach problems — the patience, the instinct, the quiet confidence of people who've seen things break in exactly the right ways enough times to know how to fix them — it changed how I think about code entirely.

I came in knowing *what* to build.
They showed me *how* to think before you build.

There's a difference. A big one.
And I'm still learning it, slowly, every day.

## Kaizer & Ashot

If Moon and Matnep are the reason I grew as a developer here, Kaizer and Ashot are the reason I didn't go insane doing it.

Great co-workers are underrated.

Not just technically great — genuinely great. The kind who catch you when you're spiraling on a bug at midnight and say the exact right thing. Who ship clean code because they respect the next person who has to read it. Who make the whole team feel slightly more capable just by showing up.

Kaizer. Ashot. I see you. Thank you.

## Qih

And then there's Qih.

My besto friendo. Bestie of besties.

The one who gets sent the unhinged messages at 3am when I've broken something beautiful. The one who responds not with judgment but with *"okay what did you touch."* The one who somehow makes debugging feel like a shared adventure instead of a personal failure.

Every developer needs a Qih in their life.

I just happen to have the original.

## What BurhanDev taught me

Beyond the stack, beyond the deployments and the late-night commits — what BurhanDev really taught me is this:

The best code comes from the best environments.
And the best environments are built by people who give a damn about each other.

I came here to level up my skills.
I'm leaving this chapter with something more valuable —

a team I'd go to war with.
